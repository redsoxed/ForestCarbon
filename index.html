The Voluntary Carbon Market is Maturing

A Market in Transition

The voluntary carbon market (VCM) is experiencing explosive growth, projected by MSCI to reach $35 billion by 2030 and $250 billion by 2050. This growth is driven by increasing scrutiny over credit quality and a clear preference for high-integrity, nature-based removal credits.

Key Trend: Carbon removal credits were 381% more expensive than reduction credits in 2024, signaling a major market shift towards quality.

Key Drivers of Demand

Indonesia's Green Future

With a planned $65 billion national green fund and a structured regulatory system (SRN), Indonesia is a high-barrier, high-upside jurisdiction where our experience provides a unique advantage.

Global Regulatory Tailwinds

Emerging Emissions Trading Systems (ETS) in Vietnam, India, and Brazil are creating new markets for high-quality carbon credits, and we are positioned to expand.

Corporate Net-Zero Action

Over 2,100 companies in the "Race to Zero" are driving demand. Companies buying credits reduce internal emissions nearly twice as fast as non-buyers.

Competitive Landscape: Peer Analysis

Permian Global

Develops jurisdictionally aligned forest conservation and restoration projects in tropical regions, focusing on REDD+ and ARR methodologies.

Mombak

"The Brazil version of Forest Carbon," Mombak converts degraded Amazon pastureland into native-species forests to generate long-term removal credits.

The Landbanking Group

Applies a methodology to price natural capital, creating a balance sheet asset through standardized digital units called Natural Capital Units (NCUs).

Revalue

A venture-backed nature technology company that designs and supports carbon projects by integrating remote sensing and data science.

Lestari (The Rimba Collective)

Offers direct supply chain decarbonization (insetting) for major CPG companies, funding projects based on their palm oil footprint.

Rubicon Carbon

A TPG-backed platform redefining climate finance with securitized, actively managed portfolios of carbon credits like the Rubicon Carbon Tonne (RCT).
