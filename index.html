Market Deep Dive: The Decisive "Flight to Quality"

The voluntary carbon market (VCM) is maturing, with a clear and growing preference for specific, high-integrity removal credits.

The Soaring Premium on Removals

In 2024, credits from removal projects commanded a massive 381% price premium over avoidance credits, up from 245% in 2023. This accelerating trend directly benefits Forest Carbon's core product.

Explosive Growth Projections

Leading analysts project exponential growth in the VCM, with demand potentially increasing 15-100x by 2050. Forest Carbon is positioned to capture a significant share of this expansion.

Removals: High Demand, Low Supply

Despite intense demand, high-quality removal credits make up a tiny fraction (5%) of the total market supply, with ARR projects comprising 99% of that slice. This scarcity creates a significant opportunity for specialized developers.

The Value of Recent Vintages

Corporate buyers pay a large premium (up to 217%) for recently issued credits. This favors developers with active project cycles who can deliver new supply under the latest, most trusted methodologies.

Powerful Market Drivers

Structural shifts in regulation, finance, and corporate strategy are creating powerful tailwinds for long-term growth.

Indonesia: Our Strategic Moat

National Green Fund: A planned $65B government fund to finance large-scale restoration, backed by carbon sales.
Regulatory Infrastructure: A national registry (SRN) and readiness for Article 6.2 of the Paris Agreement position the country as a key international player.
Operational Advantage: High barriers to entry and strong controls favor experienced local actors like Forest Carbon over more permissive but riskier jurisdictions.

Global Regulatory Convergence

Vietnam & India: Developing domestic Emissions Trading Systems (ETS), set to launch by 2029 and 2026 respectively.
Brazil: Actively building a regulated market under its Ecological Transformation Plan.
South Korea & China: Mature, large-scale ETS platforms are already operational, demonstrating the global trend toward standardized carbon pricing.

Accelerating Corporate Demand

Record Retirements: Companies retired 164M credits in 2023, up 6% YoY, showing consistent demand despite market turbulence.
Deep Decarbonization: Studies show companies buying credits reduce their own emissions nearly twice as fast as non-buyers.
Forward Agreements: Major corporations are entering long-term purchase agreements, signaling strong, durable demand for future supply.

Innovation Spotlight: The PEG Product

The Payment for Environmental Guarantees (PEG) is our answer to market fragmentation and a key differentiator, creating a new pathway for environmental finance.

A Securitized, Tranched Structure

The PEG is an over-the-counter (OTC) product that bundles different environmental assets into a single vehicle. This allows investors to select their desired level of risk and provides a monetization pathway for assets that are not yet bankable on their own, like biodiversity credits.

Lowest Risk: Top Tranche

High-integrity, verified carbon removal credits (ARR). The most mature and liquid asset class.

Medium Risk: Middle Tranche

Lower-quality removal or avoidance credits (e.g., older vintages, different methodologies). Priced lower, with higher volatility.

Highest Risk: Bottom Tranche

Biodiversity credits. Currently unbankable and illiquid, but with significant potential upside as the market develops.
