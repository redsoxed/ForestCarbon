The Voluntary Carbon Market is Maturing

A Market in Transition

The voluntary carbon market (VCM) is experiencing explosive growth, projected to reach $35 billion by 2030. This growth is driven by increasing scrutiny over credit quality and a clear preference for high-integrity, nature-based removal credits.

Key Trend: Carbon removal credits were 381% more expensive than reduction credits in 2024, signaling a major market shift towards quality.

Key Drivers of Demand

Indonesia's Green Future

With a planned $65 billion national green fund and a structured regulatory system (SRN), Indonesia is a high-barrier, high-upside jurisdiction where our experience provides a unique advantage.

Global Regulatory Tailwinds

Emerging Emissions Trading Systems (ETS) in Vietnam, India, and Brazil are creating new markets for high-quality carbon credits, and we are positioned to expand.

Corporate Net-Zero Action

Over 2,100 companies in the "Race to Zero" are driving demand. Companies buying credits reduce internal emissions nearly twice as fast as non-buyers.

Strategic Opportunities

Innovating in Environmental Finance

Strategic Diversification

While focusing on carbon removals, we are targeting 10% of revenue from non-carbon ecosystem services to hedge against market volatility.

Biodiversity Credit Strategy

Pioneering the architecture for future biodiversity markets through structured innovation, establishing us as thought leaders.

Product Innovation: The PEG

Introducing the Payment for Environmental Guarantees (PEG), a first-of-its-kind securitized product to unlock monetization for a range of environmental assets.
